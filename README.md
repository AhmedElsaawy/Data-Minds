Academic performance is influenced by many factors, including study habits, sleep patterns, and time management.
One of the most debated topics among students is whether studying during the day or at night leads to better academic results. 
Some students believe they are more productive at night due to fewer distractions, while others prefer studying during the day 
when they feel more focused and energetic.This study aims to explore the relationship between study time (day vs. night) and
academic performance among university students. Understanding this relationship can help students improve their study habits 
and achieve better academic outcomes.
